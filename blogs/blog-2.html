Beyond Kennels: Why Home-Based Pet Boarding Is Winning Hearts Across India

As pet ownership soars across India, the demand for reliable, compassionate pet care is transforming dramatically. Traditional kennel boarding, once the default choice for pet parents, is facing competition from an emerging preference: home-based pet boarding. This evolving model, where pets stay in the homes of trusted hosts rather than commercial facilities, is gaining traction in cities from Mumbai to Bengaluru to Delhi.

In a country where pets are increasingly seen as family members, Indian pet owners are seeking care options that prioritise comfort, familiarity and emotional wellbeing. Home-based boarding is proving to be more than just a convenience, it’s becoming a movement shaped by community, culture and care.

India’s Pet Care Revolution and New Priorities

The Indian pet care market is booming, projected to cross ₹10,000 crore in 2025. This growth is fuelled not only by rising pet ownership but also by changing attitudes. Today’s pet parents want premium, personalised services that treat their companions as beloved family, not just animals.

In this landscape, pet boarding has traditionally meant kennels or professional pet hotels. But many Indian pet owners are now wary of these options. Concerns about stress, overcrowding, impersonal care and hygiene drive the search for better alternatives. Home-based pet boarding meets these needs by blending safety with the warmth of a homely environment.

What is Home-Based Pet Boarding?

Home-based pet boarding means your pet stays in the house of a verified pet lover, often sharing the space with the host's own animals or receiving dedicated, personalised attention. Unlike kennels or commercial boarding centres, this model offers:

  • A cage-free, family-like atmosphere
  • Consistent care routines tailored to each pet
  • Reduced stress thanks to quieter, less crowded surroundings
  • Regular updates to owners through photos and messages

Many platforms in India, such as Pawspace, ThePetNest and PetBacker, connect pet parents with reliable hosts nearby, enabling seamless booking, verified profiles and safety assurances.

Why Indian Pet Parents Prefer Home Boarding

Several factors make home-based boarding particularly appealing across India:

  • Comfort and Familiarity: Pets adapt better to homely settings than to kennel environments, where noise, separation and unfamiliarity cause anxiety, especially for dogs and cats.
  • Safety and Health: Smaller home settings minimise exposure to contagious diseases and parasites, a key concern for pet parents in urban and semi-urban areas.
  • Personalised Care: Hosts typically offer one-on-one attention, ensuring feeding, medication and exercise happen as per the pet’s routine. This level of customised care is difficult to guarantee in big kennel setups.
  • Emotional Connection: Hosts often form genuine bonds with the pets they care for, making stays less transactional and more nurturing. Many hosts consider it a privilege and responsibility, rather than just a business.

Home-Based Boarding in Indian Cities: Case Studies

In Bengaluru, Pawspace is popular for offering cage-free, loving host homes that cater to busy professionals who need short-term boarding for their pets. One user shared how their Shih Tzu was cared for “as if their very own”, highlighting how empathy bridges the gap between pet parent and host.

In Mumbai, growing demand for home boarding reflects the stress of city life, pet owners value the calm, personalised approach hosts offer over impersonal kennels. Professional pet lovers provide flexible options tailored to festivals, vacations or work trips, closing the gap between urban lifestyles and pet care.

Similarly, in Delhi NCR, platforms enable easy discovery of pet hosts with verified backgrounds and reviews, building trust through transparency. The community formed around shared pet care activities fosters not only care but also companionship among hosts and owners.

Challenges and How They Are Being Addressed

While home-based boarding is growing rapidly, some concerns remain:

  • Consistency and Training: Ensuring all hosts meet high standards requires training and platform oversight. Many Indian platforms now provide training resources and rigorous verification.
  • Urban Space Constraints: Not every host home is spacious, which can limit pets' freedom. However, hosts often compensate with frequent walks and playtime outside the home.
  • Pricing and Accessibility: Costs can be higher than traditional kennels but many owners find the emotional benefits well worth the investment. Flexible packages are increasingly available to broaden accessibility.

    • The Broader Impact on Indian Pet Culture

    Home-based pet boarding serves as a timely response to the evolving human-animal bond in India. It reflects and reinforces a culture that values empathy, responsibility and community inclusion.

    Children from hosting families learn compassion early. Pet hosts often become part of a larger network, exchanging health tips, vets references and emergency support. This shared ecosystem strengthens the urban fabric where millions live separated yet connected by mutual care for animals.

    It also supports local economies, through demand for pet food, veterinary care, grooming services and pet-friendly urban spaces, creating ripple effects beyond the individual host households.

    Technology and Trust: Pillars of Growth

    The rise of home boarding is inseparable from technology. App-based platforms play a crucial role in connecting owners with trusted hosts, standardising quality and creating transparent feedback loops. GPS tracking, regular photo updates and background checks have become standard offerings that enable peace of mind.

    Moreover, digital communities foster narrative sharing, making pet hosting not just a service but a shared cultural experience that encourages others to join the movement.

    Heartwarming Stories from Indian Hosts and Owners

    Across India, pet hosts share inspiring stories of transformation and connection. A Delhi-based host now boards multiple dogs regularly, describing each stay as an “exchange of love and trust”. A Chennai family that started as occasional hosts ended up adopting a rescue dog who stole their hearts.

    Owners often recount how their pets return home happier and calmer, cementing loyalty to the host model. These real-life accounts highlight how home boarding is about more than just care; it’s about community, healing and shared humanity.

    Final Thoughts: Why Home-Based Pet Boarding Matters in India

    In a country where family ties run deep and community is cherished, the surge in home-based pet boarding marks a natural evolution in animal care. It aligns with Indian values of hospitality and respect while embracing modern urban realities and emotional needs.

    By moving beyond kennels, India’s pet lovers are redefining what care means; transforming guests into extended family and creating bonds that speak to the heart of community itself.

    As more Indians adopt these home-based models, the future looks bright for pets and people alike; ushering in a kinder, closer and more compassionate society.
