What Every Pet Parent Should Check Before Leaving Their Pet

By PetBnB Team • Updated November 2025

Not a substitute for professional veterinary help.

Leaving your furry friend with someone else is never easy. Whether it’s for a weekend getaway, a long business assignment or even a family emergency, the worry is always the same: Will my pet be safe, happy and cared for?

The good news is that with the right preparation, you can take much of the anxiety out of the process. Choosing a sitter is not just about convenience, it’s about ensuring your pet feels as loved and secure in someone else’s home as they do in yours. Before you hand over the leash or carrier, here are the essentials every pet parent should check.

With a Sitter

A pet’s comfort often begins with their surroundings. When evaluating a sitter, take time to look at their home environment. Are balconies secure with grills or netting? Are doors and gates properly latched? Is the space free of hazards like exposed wires, open trash bins or toxic plants?

Cleanliness is just as important. Pets are naturally curious and a cluttered or unsafe space can easily lead to accidents. By reviewing photos or, better yet, visiting in person, you can gauge whether your pet will feel relaxed and safe. A welcoming environment helps pets settle faster and reduces stress when you’re away.

Experience With Pets

Not all sitters have the same level of expertise. Some specialize in energetic puppies, others are more comfortable with cats and a few may have experience caring for senior pets with medical needs.

Ask specific questions: Have you cared for pets with separation anxiety? How do you handle medication schedules? Are you comfortable with large breeds? The more experience a sitter has, the better prepared they are to handle unexpected behavior or emergencies.

At MyPetBnB, sitter profiles highlight their background, preferences and areas of expertise, so parents can choose someone who truly understands their pet’s needs.

Routine and Lifestyle

Pets thrive on routine. A sudden change in schedule; whether it’s feeding, walks or bedtime; can cause stress. That’s why it’s essential to confirm whether the sitter can accommodate your pet’s normal lifestyle.

For example, if your dog is used to two walks a day at specific times or if your cat expects playtime before bed, make sure the sitter can match those habits. The closer the alignment, the smoother the transition will be. Consistency helps pets feel at home, even when they’re away from home.

Communication and Updates

One of the biggest worries for pet parents is not knowing how their pets are doing. Thankfully, technology makes it easier than ever to stay connected. Ask your sitter if they’re comfortable sending regular photos, videos or updates through WhatsApp or the hosting app.

On platforms like MyPetBnB, communication is built into the experience, sitters are encouraged to share updates, so parents never feel out of the loop. A quick photo of your pet snoozing or playing can make all the difference in easing your worries.

Emergency Readiness

No matter how careful we are, accidents can happen. That’s why it’s vital to check whether the sitter is prepared for emergencies. Do they know the nearest veterinary clinic? Have they dealt with medical issues before? Do they have your contact details and your pet’s medical history on hand?

A sitter with basic knowledge of pet first aid or at least a clear plan of action in case of emergencies, can make all the difference. This readiness is not just about peace of mind for you but about ensuring your pet gets immediate attention if needed.

Reviews and References

Trust isn’t built overnight, it’s earned. That’s why reviews and references are invaluable when selecting a sitter. Reading feedback from other pet parents gives you honest insights into a sitter’s reliability, attitude and care style.

Ratings and reviews on MyPetBnB make this process simple and transparent. Instead of relying on word-of-mouth alone, you can access a wealth of shared experiences to guide your decision.

End Note

A successful pet-sitting experience depends on preparation. By checking the sitter’s environment, experience, routine compatibility, communication style, emergency readiness and reviews, you’re not only protecting your pet, you’re also ensuring peace of mind for yourself.
